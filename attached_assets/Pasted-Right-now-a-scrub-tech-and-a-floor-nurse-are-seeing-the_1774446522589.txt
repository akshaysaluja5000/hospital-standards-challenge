Right now a scrub tech and a floor nurse are seeing the same content, which creates noise and reduces engagement.

The Core Concept: Role-Based Learning Tracks
Instead of one universal path, each staff member is assigned a track based on their role and department. They only see the chapters, drills, and tests relevant to their job.

MOSH Role-to-Chapter Mapping
Role	Relevant Chapters
Scrub Tech / Surgical Tech	Transport of Instruments, OR & Sterile Technique, Instruments & Inspection, Surgical Safety & Consent, SPD & Decontamination
SPD Technician	Transport of Instruments, SPD & Decontamination, Clean vs. Dirty, Sterile Storage, Instruments & Inspection
OR Nurse / Circulator	OR & Sterile Technique, Surgical Safety & Consent, Patient Care & Documentation, EOC & Safety Compliance, Facilities & Equipment
PACU / Floor Nurse	Patient Care & Documentation, EOC & Safety Compliance, Surgical Safety & Consent
OR Manager / Charge Nurse	All chapters (leadership needs full picture)
Compliance Officer / CNO	All chapters + Action Plan dashboard
Environmental Services	Environment & Surfaces, Clean vs. Dirty, EOC & Safety Compliance
Facilities/Maintenance	Facilities & Equipment, EOC & Safety Compliance, Environment & Surfaces
What Changes Structurally in the App
1. Onboarding: Role Selection
At registration or first login, the user selects their role/department. This sets their track permanently (editable by admin).

2. Personalized Dashboard
Each user sees only their assigned chapters — their handbook, drills, diagnostic, and post-test are all scoped to their role. A scrub tech never sees the PACU nursing content and vice versa.

3. Role-Specific Diagnostic & Post-Test
Instead of a single 25-question test covering all chapters:

Each role gets a diagnostic and post-test scoped to their chapters only

Question count scales with number of assigned chapters (e.g., SPD tech gets 15 questions; OR nurse gets 20)

Scores are more meaningful because they reflect actual job relevance

4. Action Plans Become Department-Scoped
When a gap is flagged, the corrective action plan goes to that department's manager — not a generic admin. An SPD gap routes to the SPD manager; an OR consent gap routes to the OR director.

SPD tech answers OR nursing questions	SPD tech focuses entirely on decon and sterile storage
Generic compliance score	Department-level compliance scores
Hard to argue ROI	Clear: "SPD is 85% ready, OR nursing is 67% — here's the action plan"
Build Complexity
This is not a major rebuild — it's primarily a data model addition:

Add a role field to the user table

Add a chapter_roles mapping table (which roles see which chapters)

Filter all content queries by the user's role

Scope diagnostic/post-test question pools to assigned chapters

Most of your existing content, quiz logic, and UI stays exactly the same — you're just adding a filter layer on top.
