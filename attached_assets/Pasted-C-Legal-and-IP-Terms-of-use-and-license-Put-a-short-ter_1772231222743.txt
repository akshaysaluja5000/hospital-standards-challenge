C. Legal and IP
Terms of use and license

Put a short terms‑of‑use page and “Not affiliated with The Joint Commission; for educational purposes only” notice.

Clarify: content cannot be resold or reproduced; access is per‑facility license.

Institutional agreements

For hospitals, you’ll likely package this as:

Subscription (SaaS) for X seats/year, or

Site license (unlimited staff at one physical facility) for a fixed annual fee.

Include data‑use language (aggregated stats for quality improvement, no patient data stored).

Branding protection

Use your own product name/branding prominently; avoid Joint Commission logos or verbatim language from their documents in the UI.

2. Monetization models tailored to hospital compliance
Here are realistic ways to charge:

Model	How it works	Fits your app because…
Per‑seat subscription	$X per user per month/year	You track users, questions answered, accuracy, streaks. Easy metering.
Department/site license	Flat annual fee per department or hospital	Simpler for OR leadership; unlimited staff within one site.
Tiered plans	Basic vs Pro (extra analytics, custom content, SSO)	Admin dashboard and reference guide lend themselves to higher tiers.
Implementation + training	One‑time setup + on‑site or virtual training	You can run a kickoff, customize question sets, and bill for that.
White‑labeling	Branded version for a system (logo/colors)	Your UI is already clean; small theme changes can be sold as an add‑on.
You can combine: e.g., “$3,000/site/year includes up to 100 users, admin dashboard, and reporting; extra sites or custom modules are add‑ons.”
