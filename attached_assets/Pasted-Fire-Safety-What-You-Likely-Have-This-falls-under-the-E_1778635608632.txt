Emergency Management — What You're Missing
This is an entirely separate JCAHO chapter — Emergency Management (EM). It covers:

EM.01.01.01 — The facility has an Emergency Operations Plan (EOP)
EM.02.01.01 — Hazard Vulnerability Analysis (HVA) — identifying what disasters could affect the facility
EM.02.02.01 — The six critical areas of the EOP: communications, resources, safety/security, utilities, clinical activities, staff responsibilities
EM.03.01.01 — Drills and exercises — hospitals must conduct two emergency drills per year
EM.04.01.01 — After-action reviews following drills or actual emergencies
Staff roles and responsibilities during a declared emergency
Shelter in place vs. evacuation decisions
Surge capacity planning
Utility failures during emergencies
Community integration — how the facility coordinates with local emergency management


Why This Matters for SP Specifically
Surgery Partners operates facilities in multiple states and regions. Enterprise health systems get cited on Emergency Management more than almost any other chapter because:

The HVA must be facility-specific and updated annually
Drill documentation requirements are strict
Staff must know their specific role during an emergency — not just general awareness
Surveyors will ask frontline staff about the EOP directly

This is exactly the kind of gap that a compliance officer like Tamala would notice immediately.
