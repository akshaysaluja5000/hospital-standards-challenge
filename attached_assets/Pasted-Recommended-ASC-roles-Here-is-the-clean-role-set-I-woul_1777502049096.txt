Recommended ASC roles
Here is the clean role set I would use for the Ambulatory Surgery Center section.

Leadership & Compliance
ASC Administrator

Medical Director

Director of Nursing / Clinical Director

Quality / Infection Prevention Coordinator

Nurse Educator / Staff Development (only if the center truly has one; many smaller ASCs won’t)

These roles match how ASCs are typically led: administrator for overall operations, medical director for physician/clinical oversight, and a nursing or clinical director for day-to-day clinical execution and standards compliance.

Front Office / Patient Access
Front Desk / Registration

Scheduling / Authorization Coordinator

Medical Records / Health Information

Smaller ASCs often combine these functions, so if the center is small, you can merge them into Front Office / Scheduling / Registration rather than pretending they have hospital-style departmental separation.

Pre-Op / PACU
Pre-Op / PACU Nurse

PACU Charge Nurse / Perioperative Supervisor (if applicable)

Do not use “PACU / Floor Nurse” for an ASC unless the center actually has overnight/floor capability. Most ASCs are not hospitals and do not have floor nurses in the inpatient sense.

Operating Room
OR Circulating Nurse

Scrub Tech / Surgical Tech

OR Manager / Charge Nurse

Surgical Assistant / Orthopedic Assistant (if relevant to the cases you do)

Those are fine and should stay close to what you already have.

Sterile Processing
SPD Technician

Sterile Processing Lead / Manager (optional if the center is large enough)

Sterile processing is important enough in ASCs that it deserves its own role set, especially because equipment/instrument flow and sterility failures are a huge compliance risk.

Facilities / Environment
Environmental Services

Facilities / Maintenance

These can stay, though in some ASCs they may be outsourced or combined. If outsourced, you may want a role like Contracted Facilities / EVS Support later.

Credentialing / Business Office
Credentialing / Medical Staff Coordinator

Billing / Business Office

Materials / Supply Coordinator (optional but useful in surgery centers)

Credentialing matters more in ASCs than many people realize because privileging, medical staff files, NPDB checks, reappointments, and physician documentation are major operational issues.

What I would rename from your current hospital list
A few of your current names should change for ASC:

Current hospital-style name	Better ASC name
Compliance Officer / CNO	ASC Administrator / Clinical Leadership or split into ASC Administrator and Director of Nursing / Clinical Director
PACU / Floor Nurse	Pre-Op / PACU Nurse
Nurse Educator / Staff Development	Keep only if real; otherwise remove
Facilities / Maintenance	Keep
Environmental Services	Keep
OR Manager / Charge Nurse	Keep
Surgical/Orthopedic Assistant	Keep if actually used
SPD Technician	Keep
The big mistake would be keeping a hospital-style CNO label in an ASC unless the actual structure really uses that title.

That is enough to look real without bloating the UI.

Access logic
I would assign access like this:

Full Access: ASC Administrator

Dept + All: Medical Director, Director of Nursing / Clinical Director, Quality / Infection Prevention Coordinator, OR Manager / Charge Nurse

Dept Access: everyone else

That mirrors actual oversight patterns better than pretending every support role needs cross-facility visibility.

Blunt recommendation
Do not overload the first version with 25 micro-roles. That’s fake precision. Start with 15–17 real roles that match how ASCs actually function, then split roles later only if pilots show a need.
